Tactics
The element cycle
Your core pattern: generate [fire] + [light] in the same round, then spend both on Shield of the Desert TOP next round — attack 4 on 2 targets. Elements must be spent the round immediately after generation or they go inert. Strong generate combinations: Flaming Sickle TOP + Shocking Advance BOT; Harvest Sickle TOP + Shield Spikes BOT; Radiant Sickle TOP + Shield Spikes BOT (attacks while generating both).
Strangling Chain
Best on elites and bosses. Play the TOP early (initiative 19 is fast) so every attack on the target that round triggers suffer damage — including the Demolitionist's. Consuming [fire] raises that from 1 to 2 per hit. The {immobilize} keeps the target from running so nobody has to chase it.
Healing Sands BOT
No persistent shield in this build. When you're being hit hard, Healing Sands BOT (all attacks against you gain disadvantage this round) is your main mitigation. Use the TOP sparingly — heal 4 is powerful, but it costs the card for the round.
Flame Shroud BOT
Your fastest reliable move (Move 4). In this build, use the BOT in almost all cases — the TOP's passive damage rarely justifies losing the mobility. The BOT also lets you consume available [fire] for a Shield 1 bonus if you need it.
Radiant Sickle
TOP is your strongest no-setup attack — Attack 4 at range 2, plus free suffer 1 damage to any enemies adjacent to your target (goes through shields). Also creates [light] for the element cycle. BOT: Heal 2 at range 2 with a move — use this to keep the Demolitionist healthy. You do not need to be adjacent to reach them.
Desert Night TOP
Disarm cancels a dangerous enemy's next attack. The disarm is a separate effect from the melee attack — it lands even if your attack misses. Best played before a hard-hitting enemy acts. The [ice] + [dark] go unused in this party.
Combos
Element Cycle
Round N: generate [fire] + [light] in the same round. Standard: Flaming Sickle TOP + Shocking Advance BOT. New option: Radiant Sickle TOP + Shield Spikes BOT — attacks for 4 and heals while generating both elements. Round N+1: Shield of the Desert TOP consumes both → attack 4 on 2 targets. Aim for this every other round.
Strangling Chain Setup
Play Strangling Chain TOP (initiative 19) before enemies act. The Demolitionist attacks the {immobilize}d target later that round — each hit triggers suffer 1 (or 2 if you consumed [fire]). The earlier in the round you play it, the more attacks it multiplies.
Watch Out For
Shield of Desert BOT
Never play the bottom. It is a [BURN] — using it permanently removes your most powerful attack card from the scenario. One round of Shield 3 is never worth that cost.
Shield Spikes TOP
In this build, never activate the TOP. It is [PERM] — once played it permanently occupies your active area and cannot be recovered with a stamina potion. It provides no benefit in a DPS build.
Strangling Chain timing
The suffer damage is round-only — if you play it late, there are few or no follow-up attacks left to benefit. Initiative 19 usually gets you in front of enemies; don't hold the card for the end of a round.
