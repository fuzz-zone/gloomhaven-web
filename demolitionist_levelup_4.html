Leveling up isn't just picking a card. You can also rework your hand — swap out earlier choices, bring back cards you skipped. Use this as a chance to step back and ask: is the Demolitionist playing the way I want?

This guide walks through the new cards, two directions you could go, and how the swap rule works.

The Two New Cards
At level 4 you choose one of these to add to your card pool. (You don't have to use it every scenario, but you'd own it.)
Extra Fuel
Initiative 17  ·  one of your fastest
[TOP] Attack 3; +2 attack and {wound} if the target is next to a wall (attack 5 total). Creates [fire].
[BOT] Move 5; consume [fire] → attack 1 at the end of the move.
A stronger version of Crushing Weight, plus the best move card you've ever had. Reliable, no setup needed.
Remote Detonation
Initiative 63  ·  slow
[TOP] Destroy an obstacle within range 4; attack 2 to all enemies next to that obstacle. Consume [fire] → attack 3.
[BOT] [BURN] Create three 3-damage traps in empty hexes within range 4.
A "blow stuff up from range" card. Big payoff when obstacles are conveniently placed, less useful when they aren't.
Two Directions You Could Take
Your current hand leans toward walls and stunning. Level 4 is a good moment to either double down on that, or pivot to something different. Both are legitimate — pick what sounds fun.
A. Keep Going: Walls & Booms
Your current direction

You smash enemies into walls, stun the dangerous ones with Robotic Enhancement, and clear groups with Firebombs. Extra Fuel fits straight into this — it's a stronger Crushing Weight with a big move on the bottom.

Steady and reliable. Works regardless of scenario layout. Doesn't need much setup or planning.

L4 hand suggestion: swap The Big One (you rarely play it) for Extra Fuel. Everything else stays.
B. Pivot: Burst Damage
Bigger change, bigger ceiling

A different style: line up a single huge hit on one enemy by stacking Wind Up (a "charge up" card) with your wall attacks. Trade reliable stunning for explosive burst.

Higher damage ceiling — Crushing Weight + Wind Up + Extra Fuel against a wall can hit for 7–9 damage in one swing. But more setup-dependent, and you give up some stunning safety.

Requires multiple swaps — see "Reshaping Your Hand" below.
Reshaping Your Hand (Mulligans)
The house rule
At our table you can swap any past card choice when you level up. Took Robotic Enhancement at level 2? You can give it back and take Crash Protocol instead. Took Firebombs at level 3? You can swap it for Nowhere to Run. Nothing is locked in.
Always available
The three "X" cards — Level, Lobbed Charge, and Rubble — are sideboard cards you can swap in or out for any scenario, regardless of level. Worth checking each scenario to see if one fits.
Path A swaps
Just one: drop The Big One, add Extra Fuel. That's it. Your hand keeps the same shape.
Path B swaps

Bigger restructuring. To pivot into the burst style:

• Bring back Wind Up (you skipped it at level 1).
• Swap Robotic Enhancement → Crash Protocol at level 2.
• Swap Firebombs → Nowhere to Run at level 3.
• Add Extra Fuel at level 4.
• Drop the cards that no longer fit (Explosive Blitz, Knockout the Support, Implode all leave the hand).

This is a real commitment — you're rebuilding most of the hand. Worth it if the current style isn't clicking, less worth it if it is.

If you're enjoying how the Demolitionist plays right now, Path A is the easy call. Extra Fuel makes your strongest combo even stronger, drops a card you rarely use (The Big One), and you don't have to relearn anything.

If something feels off — maybe stunning isn't doing it for you, maybe you want bigger single hits, maybe the current hand feels too situational — Path B is a real option. Speak up; we can walk through what changes feel like in play before committing.

Either way, the X-card sideboarding (Level / Lobbed Charge / Rubble) is worth using more often. Check each scenario.
